In addition to the aforementioned consequences, what happens if you fail a CI Polygraph?

A failed counterintelligence polygraph is not grounds for dismissal; rather, it serves as a starting point for an inquiry to determine whether or not there was a security violation. However, because to the vast spectrum of human responses, mistakes do occur — both false positives and false negatives.

People have also inquired as to what a CI Polygraph is.

For security clearance purposes, the CI Polygraph is the most common type of polygraph examination that is used. It is designed to screen for security issues such as those associated with spying on the government or foreign governments or terrorist activity, unauthorised disclosure of classified information, unauthorised contacts with foreign nationals, and deliberate deception.

Do you submit to a polygraph examination for top-secret clearance?

Typically, top-secret clearances do not need the use of a polygraph. Only particular occupations within the clearance level may need the use of these documents. The sort of polygraph you’re obliged to take would also go into your decision.

What should I anticipate from a polygraph examination?

A normal polygraph examination will comprise three phases: a pre-test interview, a chart gathering phase, and a test data analysis phase. A pre-test interview is the first of these phases.
