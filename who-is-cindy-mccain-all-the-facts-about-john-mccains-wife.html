Who Is Cindy McCain? All The Facts About John McCains Wife

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, September 8, 2024

American entrepreneur, philanthropist, and humanitarian, Cindy McCain is well-known for being the late Senator John McCain’s wife as well as for her involvement with numerous humanitarian organizations. We shall discuss Cindy McCain’s background, personal life, career accomplishments, and charitable activities in detail in this article.

If you do not know anything about her, this article provides all the details you need in order to get familiar with the popular wife of the former US Senator, John McCain.

Early Life and Education

On May 20, 1954, Cindy McCain was born in Phoenix, Arizona.

As of the writing of this article, Cindy was 68 years old.

She was raised in a prosperous household and went to Central High School in Phoenix.

She attended the University of Southern California after finishing high school, where she majored in education and received a Bachelor of Arts degree.

Later on, she attended the University of Arizona to earn a Master of Arts in special education.

John McCain, a former Navy pilot and U.S. senator from Arizona got married to Cindy in 1980.

The four kids born to the couple were named Meghan McCain, John Sidney McCain IV, Bridget McCain, and James McCain.

John and Cindy McCain were married for 38 years before John’s passing in 2018.

Cindy McCain’s Professional Achievements

Cindy McCain is a successful entrepreneur who has worked in a number of different fields.

She began her career as a teacher and spent several years working in the special education sector.

She then made the shift to business and worked for organizations like Anheuser-Busch and her family’s beer distributorship, Hensley & Co.

Cindy McCain rose to prominence as one of Arizona’s most powerful businesswomen in 2000 when she was appointed head of Hensley & Co.

She guided the business through a time of development and growth, assisting in its emergence as the top beer distributor in the Southwest.

Meghan McCain Philanthropy Work

Cindy McCain is well-known for her humanitarian work and has contributed to numerous nonprofits over the course of her career.

She has been a steadfast supporter of causes like refugees, drug addiction, and human trafficking.

Cindy McCain was chosen in 2018 to join the McCain Institute for International Leadership, a nonpartisan think tank that prioritizes advancing democracy, human rights, and international leadership.

She is also a member of the HALO Trust, which works to remove landmines and unexploded ordnance from combat zones, and the Arizona Human Trafficking Council.

She has also been active in a number of other organizations.

Along with her charitable endeavors, Cindy McCain has actively supported her husband’s political career.

She was an important part of his 2008 presidential campaign and has been a strong supporter of a number of political concerns, such as campaign finance and immigration reform.
