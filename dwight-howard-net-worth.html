Lifestyle

Dwight Howard has had an impact and enthusiasm for basketball since he was a youngster, born into a family with an athletic background in basketball.

Dwight Sr., his father, was the Athletic Administrator of Southwest Atlanta Christian Academy, the country’s best national high school basketball teams and infrastructures.

Similarly, his mother, Sheryl Howard, was a basketball athlete who played on the first women’s basketball team at Morris Brown College.

Having followed in his parents’ footsteps, Dwight became concerned about basketball around nine and entered SACA (Southwest Atlanta Christian Academy) to further his career.

Throughout Dwight Howard’s high school career, he was a power forward with an outstanding record.

The NBA star Howard was eventually named the finest high school basketball player in the country, and he appeared to be headed for a future in the NBA.

Evidently, Howard scored 16.6 points and 13.4 rebounds per game as a power forward while leading his high school to a 31-2 victory.

As a result, Dwight Howard led his team to the High school state title.

Howard has always been a devoted Christian since his childhood, and he has stated that he wants to utilize his NBA career and Christian religion to help promote the name of God in the league and the world.

Career

Orlando Magic (2004-2012)

Howard bypassed college and went directly to the NBA since he really was a bright young prospect during his high school years.

Further, He was drafted by the Orlando Magic in 2004 and joined a team as Magics were struggling in NBA.

In fact, he demonstrated his value by setting new milestones, such as being the NBA’s youngest player to record 20 rebounds in a match.

Subsequently, He was also named to the NBA All-Rookie Team in 2005.

In the 2006-07 season, he was named to his first All-Star Team and chosen to the All-NBA Third Team.

In addition, after leading the Orlando Magic to the NBA Finals in 2008-09, Dwight earned the NBA’s youngest Defensive Player of the Year.

Later that season, he was ranked first in the NBA for Rebounds and Blocks for the second time in a row.

Following that, in the 2010-11 season, Howard became the first player in NBA history to be voted Best Defensive Player of the Year third time in succession.

Even though the Orlando Magic were suddenly thriving, he asked to be transferred from the franchise.

Los Angeles Lakers (2012-2013)

His 2012 season was one of his first setbacks, which were mostly due to his terrible injuries.

Despite a poor season, he led the NBA in rebounding and therefore earned yet another All-NBA-Third Team honor.

Houston Rockets (2013-2016)

Dwight endured a rocky path during his three seasons with the Houston Rockets.

However, he accomplished few accomplishments, including All NBA-Second Team honors and hitting 15,000 career points in December 2015.

Whereas only reaching it to the finals once for the Rockets, Dwight also had a serious knee injury that sidelined him for four months from December 14 to March 15 of the 2014-15 campaign.

Atlanta Hawks (2016-2017)

Dwight had a terrific season with the Hawks after signing a three-year contract and got the most rebounds by any teammates in their opening match with the Atlanta Hawks.

Despite having a good season, the athlete was moved to the Charlotte Hornets the next year.

Charlotte Hornets (2017-2018)

Beginning to set new marks from the minute he arrived in June 2017, Dwight became the eighth league player in the NBA history to have a 30-30 point game.

Further, He also joined an exceptional group of six players who scored a double-double during their first 13 NBA seasons. He was moved again in July 2018.

Washington Wizards (2018–2019)

While the new season provided him with new opportunities, it also provided him with a slew of injuries to deal with.

In addition, he spent a season with the Wizards in April 2019 until being moved again in July 2019.

Los Angeles Lakers (2019–2020)

Further, Howard rejoined the L.A. Lakers on a $2.6 million contract.

Charity Works

The NBA star Dwight Howard is very involved in charities and contributing to society.

In 2004, Howard created his own charity foundation named Dwight D. Howard (D12) Foundation.

The foundation’s goal is to provide scholarships to kids interested in attending his college in Southwest Atlanta Christian Academy.

Meanwhile, it provides funds to Orlando, Florida’s Lovell Elementary School, and Memorial Middle School.

In addition, the NBA player runs summertime basketball programs for both boys and girls, working with college and high school coaches.

Furthermore, he invites his other NBA players to attend the camps.
