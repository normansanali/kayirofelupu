Dean Cain from 'Lois &amp; Clark: The New Adventures of Superman' Appears in Public: 'He Still Looks Goo

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, July 10, 2024
  • Dean Cain rose to fame as Superman in the mid-'90s series "Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman."
  • Transitioning beyond Hollywood, he became a reserve police officer.
  • In 2023, his appearance at the Hollywood Christmas Parade garnered admiration from fans.

Dean Cain as Superman on "Lois and Clark: The New Adventures of Superman" on November 17, 2008. | Source: Getty Images

Dean Cain became a household name when he starred in the mid-'90s series "Lois and Clark: The New Adventures of Superman." The actor portrayed Superman in the popular television series, which ran from 1993 to 1997 on ABC, bringing his charismatic and charming demeanor to the character.

Teri Hatcher and Dean Cain on "Lois and Clark: The New Adventures of Superman" on August 16, 1994. | Source: Getty Images

Cain's performance in the series, which lasted four seasons, contributed to the show's success, earning him acclaim for his portrayal of the iconic DC Comics character. After "Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman" ended, Dean Cain continued to work in the entertainment industry.

Dean Cain during HBO's Comic Relief VII Benefit Homeless at Universal Amphitheatre in Universal City, California on November 11, 1995. | Source: Getty Images

The actor starred in productions such as "Ripley's Believe It or Not!" and "The Perfect Husband: The Laci Peterson Story." In addition to his acting career, Cain ventured into hosting, documentary narration, and reality television.

Cain made a comeback in the entertainment scene when he attended the 91st Hollywood Christmas Parade.

Dean Cain attends the Calabasas Motor Cars Celebrity golf tournament, held in Calabasas, California, on June 30, 1997. | Source: Getty Images

The movie star has remained active in the public eye, especially when he was sworn in as a police officer in 2018. On June 19, 2018, the Saint Anthony Police Department in Idaho announced that Cain had joined the police:

"St. Anthony PD swears in Reserve Officer Dean Cain who played Superman in the TV series Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman."

Dean Cain speaks onstage during the 24th Family Film Awards in Universal City, California, on March 24, 2021. | Source: Getty Images

Cain was pictured next to Captain Anderson, who had sworn him in, looking happy with a broad smile. The photos showed the actor being sworn in and proudly showing off his certificate. He admitted to always feeling a connection with the military and first responders, and so joining their ranks was his way of contributing and playing a part.

"I have always been a man of action."

Dean Cain attends the Algemeiner J100 Gala at Capitale Bowery in New York City on October 25, 2023. | Source: Getty Images

The actor became part of the "All About Kids" initiative when he joined the police. The initiative addresses challenges like online bullying and predation faced by children and teens, aiming to help them handle these issues effectively.

Dean Cain attends the 91st anniversary of the Hollywood Christmas Parade, supporting Marine Toys For Tots in Hollywood, California, on November 26, 2023. | Source: Getty Images

Anna Azerli and Dean Cain attend the 91st anniversary of the Hollywood Christmas Parade, supporting Marine Toys For Tots in Hollywood, California, on November 26, 2023. | Source: Getty Images

On November 26, 2023, Cain made a comeback in the entertainment scene when he attended the 91st Hollywood Christmas Parade. The actor wore a black suit and white shirt spruced up with a pink tie. He posed alone and then with actress Anna Azerli before getting into the venue.

Fans who saw Cain's pictures said, "he still looks good." They noted that the actor was "handsome" in his suit. "I didn't recognize him I admit….looks good for 57," a fan acknowledged. Fans said Cain had gained weight, which has left him "unrecognizable," but in a good way.

Dean Cain attends the 91st anniversary of the Hollywood Christmas Parade, supporting Marine Toys For Tots in Hollywood, California, on November 26, 2023. | Source: Getty Images

Cain has been busy as a police officer. He has appeared in magazines such as Time Magazine defending police officers by saying that cops are not inherently violent. Cain seems to have found his calling after he left the Hollywood scene.
