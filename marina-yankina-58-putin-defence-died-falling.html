Marina Yankina Case Update

One of the Russian top defense officials has been found dead and is believed to have fallen 160ft after plunging from a high window in a tower block. 

A passerby discovered 58-year-old Marina Yankina at the entrance of a House on Zamshina Street, St Petersburg.

According to the Daily Mail, Yankina led the financial support department of the Ministry of Defence.

In short, she was the head of the finance department for the Western Military District.

Also, the Western Military District was closely involved in the dictator’s invasion.

Previously, Yankina worked in the Federal Tax Service. 

Before joining the Western Military region, the high-ranking official also served as St. Petersburg’s Deputy Chairman of the Property Relations Committee.

Now, the initial report suspects suicide, but often such cases present these facts prior main investigation.

Verified sources such as The Sun, the Russian Investigative Committee, and the Western Military District press ‘Fontanka,‘ confirmed Yankina’s death. However, the committee is still probing her mystery fall.

The concerned authorities early reports conflicted over whether the late official lived or worked in the St Petersburg building.

Also, officers discovered Yankina’s personal belongings on the 16th floor. So they assumed that she had fallen from there.

Also, Marina was considered friendly with senior officials in the Leningrad region.

Reportedly, the late senior officer was close to the former Ministry of Defence Chief of Staff, Mikhail Mokretsov. He was also Deputy Minister of Defence.

State official Mokretsov is a Russian businessman. In 2007, he took on as the Director of the Federal Taxation Service and ended his term in 2010.

According to the latest data, the deceased woman had health problems but the motives needs to be clarified.
