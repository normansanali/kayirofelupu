Jennifer Coolidge Career

Before gaining recognition, she appeared in such films as A Bucket of Blood, Plump Fiction, and A Night at the Roxbury.  She had a recurring role on King of the Hill, where she voiced Luanne’s beauty school teacher, Miss Kremer. In 1999, Coolidge got her big break in American Pie with the role of Jeanine Stifler, or “Stifler’s mom”.

In 2001, she repeated her role in American Pie 2. She later had a supporting role in Legally Blonde as Paulette the manicurist. In 2003, she repeated her role in Legally Blonde 2: Red, White & Blonde. That same year, she again played Jeanine Stifler in American Wedding. She also played the protagonist’s agent Luise in Testosterone.

In 2006, she guest-starred on an episode of Top Chef, and in the comedy film Click, she played as Adam Sandler’s wife’s friend, Janine. In 2007, she received a starring role in Epic Movie, this was the first movie in which she received a starring role. She also appeared on Thank God You’re Here and The Closer. In 2008, she guest-starred on The Secret Life of the American Teenager as a call girl. (now playing the fiance of Ben’s dad and future stepmother of Ben.) Living Proof and Soul Men.

Coolidge played a sensational role in Bad Lieutenant: Port of Call New Orleans as Genevieve McDonagh in 2009. She also starred alongside Heather Graham and Amber Heard in ExTerminators. In 2010, Coolidge appeared in another film starring Hilary Duff titled Beauty & the Briefcase. In October 2011, Coolidge began a recurring role in the CBS sitcom 2 Broke Girls as Sophie Kaczynski, a Polish neighbor of the two lead characters. She was later promoted to the main cast from season 2 up until the show’s cancellation in 2017.

In 2016, she had a voice role in Hell and Back, and in Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Road Chip. In 2017, she lent her voice to portray Mary Meh in The Emoji Movie. In 2018,  she made an appearance in Ariana Grande’s music video for her song Thank U, Next.

In popular culture

Due to her role in Legally Blonde, Coolidge’s voice is the subject of a popular impression of the actress, phrases often said when impersonating her include; “You look like the fourth of July!” and “Makes me wanna hot dog real bad….”.
