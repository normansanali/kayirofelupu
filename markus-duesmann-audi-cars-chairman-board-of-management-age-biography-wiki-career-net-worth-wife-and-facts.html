Markus Duesmann’s Career and Profession

Markus Duesmann was born on June 23, 1969, in Heek, North Rhine-Westphalia. After finishing his mechanical engineering studies at Muenster University of Applied Sciences, the retiring Audi CEO got an engineering degree in 1991.

Mr. Duesmann joined Mercedes-Benz in Stuttgart in 1992 as a design engineer for a V12 series-production engine.

In 1995, he moved to the Aachen, Germany-based development service provider FEV GmbH. Where he held many positions, the most recent of which was head of the engine mechanics division.

In 2004, he was named main department manager for new diesel engines at DaimlerChrysler AG in Stuttgart. He was appointed head of Formula One development at Mercedes-Benz in Brixworth, England, in 2005.

Duesmann joined BMW AG in 2007 as the company’s head of Formula 1 powertrain. He held numerous accountable positions in that company before becoming a member of the BMW AG Board of Management for Purchasing and Supplier Network.
